\section{Monads}

No self-respecting wannabe category theorist would leave out a description of monads.
In this section, a comprehensive overview of monads will be given, with the Kleisli and Eilenberg-Moore categories defined in detail.
Much of this comes directly from Mac Lane, and will begin with a quick remark on composeability of functors and natural transformations.\\

For a category $\mathbb{ A} $, and endofunctor $T:\mathbb{A} \to \mathbb{A}$ there are composites $T^2 = T \circ T$ and $T^3 = T^2 \circ T$. 
If, additionally, there is a natural transformation $\mu: T^2\to T$ then, for each $ a $ in $\mathbb{ A} $, the natural transformations $T\mu, \mu T: T^3\to T^2 $ have, respectively components $(T\mu)_a= T\mu _a$, and $(\mu T)_a = \mu_{Ta} $ .

\begin{defn} \cite{MaclCT}
A \textit{monad} $T=( T,\eta, \mu ) $ in a category $\mathbb{A}$ consists of a functor $T:\mathbb{A}\longrightarrow\mathbb{A}$ and two natural transformations
\begin{align*}
\eta:1_\mathbb{A}\longrightarrow T, \quad \mu: T^2\longrightarrow T
\end{align*}
such that the following diagrams commute:
\[\begin{tikzcd}
{T^3} & {T^2} & 1T & {T^2} & T1 & (1)\\
{T^2} & T & T & T & T
\arrow["{T\mu}", from=1-1, to=1-2]
\arrow["{\mu T}"', from=1-1, to=2-1]
\arrow["\mu", from=1-2, to=2-2]
\arrow["{\eta T}", from=1-3, to=1-4]
\arrow["{||}"{description}, draw=none, from=1-3, to=2-3]
\arrow["\mu"', from=1-4, to=2-4]
\arrow["{T \eta}"', from=1-5, to=1-4]
\arrow["{||}"{description}, draw=none, from=1-5, to=2-5]
\arrow["\mu"', from=2-1, to=2-2]
\arrow["{=}"{description}, draw=none, from=2-3, to=2-4]
\arrow["{=}"{description}, draw=none, from=2-4, to=2-5]
\end{tikzcd}\]
\end{defn}
